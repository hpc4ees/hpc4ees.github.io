About the Workshop

Workshop Topics

The HPC4EES workshop invites submissions covering a broad range of topics at the intersection of high-performance computing, AI, and environmental and Earth sciences, with particular emphasis on scalable modeling, robust simulation and forecasting, and reliable, efficient, and reproducible computational methods..

High-Performance Computing for Environmental and Earth Sciences

  • Large-scale weather and climate modeling and forecasting, including climate change and extreme events
  • HPC-enabled atmospheric modeling, including chemistry, air quality, and air-pollution simulations
  • Scalable Earth-system modeling, including ocean, coastal, cryosphere, and coupled atmo- sphere–land–ocean systems
  • High-performance geophysical and hazard modeling, including seismology, ground-motion, and disaster prediction
  • Scalable hydrology and water-resource modeling, including flood forecasting and risk as- sessment
  • Large-scale AI, machine learning, and statistical methods for environmental and Earth science applications
  • Digital twins and emerging HPC technologies for environmental sustainability and Earth- system science
  • Scalable numerical algorithms, solvers, and uncertainty quantification methods for envi- ronmental and Earth science applications
  • Performance portability, workflow design, and energy-efficient computing for large-scale environmental HPC workloads

Organizing Committee

Dr. Sameh Abdulah

Senior Research Scientist (KAUST)

Dr. Sameh Abdulah is a Senior Research Scientist in High-Performance Computing (HPC) at the Computer, Electrical and Mathematical Sciences and Engineering Division at KAUST, specializing in scalable algorithms and software frameworks for large-scale data analytics, spatial statistics, and climate and weather applications. He plays a key integrative role within KAUST’s Extreme Computing Research Center, bridging advanced parallel linear algebra and statistical modeling across the HiCMA, STSDS, and Environmental Statistics groups. He is a recipient of the ACM Gordon Bell Prize for Climate Modeling (2024) and was part of the KAUST team nominated for the prize in 2022, with contributions spanning mixed-precision computing, scalable matrix methods, and geostatistical modeling. In addition to his research, he actively serves the High-Performance Statistical Computing (HPSC) community by leading community-building initiatives, organizing seminars and workshops, and promoting open-source, reproducible HPC-driven statistical software.
