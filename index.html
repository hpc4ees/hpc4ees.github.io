About the Workshop

"AI for Resilient, Responsible DeFi Dynamics (HPC4EES)" addresses the critical need for advanced AI solutions in the rapidly evolving Decentralized Finance (DeFi) ecosystem.

While DeFi promises transparency and efficiency, its inherent volatility, composability risks, and smart contract vulnerabilities introduce complex challenges demanding robust analytical and decision-making capabilities.

AI is indispensable for navigating many of these unique DeFi dynamics, offering the potential to build a more resilient and responsible future. Our workshop will facilitate scholarly conversations on AI's transformative role in DeFi through enhanced risk mitigation, real-time systemic resilience, the development of intelligent and autonomous governance models, and by providing deeper insights into market dynamics to optimize efficiency.

With this dedicated and integrated focus, the HPC4EES workshopfills a crucial gap at IEEE ICBC 2026, providing a timely forum for researchers and practitioners to explore how AI can ensure robust and stable decentralized financial systems.

Workshop Topics

The HPC4EES workshop plans to invite submissions on a wide range of topics related to the application of AI in Decentralized Finance (DeFi), with a particular emphasis on robustness, stability, and ethical considerations.

High-Performance Computing for Environmental and Earth Sciences

  • Large-scale weather and climate modeling and forecasting, including climate change and extreme events
  • HPC-enabled atmospheric modeling, including chemistry, air quality, and air-pollution simulations
  • Scalable Earth-system modeling, including ocean, coastal, cryosphere, and coupled atmo- sphere–land–ocean systems
  • High-performance geophysical and hazard modeling, including seismology, ground-motion, and disaster prediction
  • Scalable hydrology and water-resource modeling, including flood forecasting and risk as- sessment
  • Large-scale AI, machine learning, and statistical methods for environmental and Earth science applications
  • Digital twins and emerging HPC technologies for environmental sustainability and Earth- system science
  • Scalable numerical algorithms, solvers, and uncertainty quantification methods for envi- ronmental and Earth science applications
  • Performance portability, workflow design, and energy-efficient computing for large-scale environmental HPC workloads

Organizing Committee

Dr. Oshani Seneviratne

Assistant Professor of Computer Science, RPI

Dr. Seneviratne leads the Bridging Resilient Accountable Intelligent Networked Systems (BRAINS) Lab. Her research focuses on decentralized systems, including web technologies, blockchain, and decentralized learning, with applications in health informatics and fintech. She has extensive workshop-organization experience including the AIChain Workshop Series at the IEEE Blockchain conference, and she was the primary organizer of the inaugural HPC4EES workshop at ACM International Conference on AI and Finance (ICAIF) in November 2025.
